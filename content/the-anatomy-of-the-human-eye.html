Lets walk through what each parts function is

Term Function
Pupil Lets light into your eye as the muscles of your iris change its shape.
Iris Muscles in your iris control your pupil. When your pupil is wider (dilated), more light gets into your eye. When it's narrower (contracted) less light gets in.
Cornea Protects the structure inside the eye, such as the pupil (the opening at the center of the eye), iris (the colored part of the eye), and anterior chamber (the fluid filled inside the eye).
Crystalline Lens The main optical function of the lens is to transmit light, focusing it on the retina. The cornea contributes about 80% of total refraction, while the Crystalline Lens fine tunes the focusing of light onto the retina.
Ciliary Body Responsible for production of the aqueous humor.
Aqueous Humor (Anterior Chamber) Located between cornea and iris. Gives the eye its shape and nourishes the cornea and lens by supplying nutrition such as amino acids and glucose.
Canal of Schlemm Collects the aqueous humor from the anterior chamber of the eyeball and delivers it to the veins of the eyeball.
Sclera Functions as the supporting wall of the eyeball. It helps maintain your eyeball's shape, and protect it from injury.
Choroid Its filled with blood vessels that bring oxygen and nutrients to the eye.
Retina Captures incoming photons and transmits them along neuronal pathways as both electrical and chemical signals for the brain to perceive a visual picture.
Fovea Centralis Responsible for high-acuity vision, it is densely saturated with cone photoreceptors.
Optic Nerve Relays messages from your eyes to your brain to create visual images.
Suspensory Ligaments Function is to change the shape of the crystalline lens within the eye.
Macula Its function is to process harp, clear, straight-ahead vision.
Vitreous Humor Function is to maintain the round shape of the eye.
Aqueous Humor (Posterior Chamber) Located between the iris, zonule fibers and crystalline lens. Function is the production and circulation of aqueous humor.
